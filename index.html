Why early Ethnic Studies?

Children begin developing ideas about race, belonging, and identity very early. Introducing Ethnic Studies at a young age helps children form broader, kinder worldviews and develops critical thinking. This project offers accessible lessons, stories, and activities that center representation and empathy.

What you’ll find on this site:
  • Interactive stories and listening activities
  • Age-appropriate lessons about identity and culture
  • Art and reflection prompts for children and families
  • Curriculum resources (educator section in development)

Get involved

Share stories, suggest resources, or contribute activities. This site is built to be collaborative—educators, families, and community members can help shape content that reflects many voices.
